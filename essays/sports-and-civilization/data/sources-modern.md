## Viewership & Broadcasting

### 4. Wikipedia: "Broadcasting of sports events"
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sports_broadcasting
- **Key data:**
  - First sports broadcast: 1899 (Marconi, America's Cup)
  - First radio sports broadcast: 1921 (boxing, Pittsburgh)
  - First live TV sport: 1931 (Epsom Derby, UK)
  - First color TV sport: 1951 (baseball, Dodgers vs. Braves)
  - BBC was prevented from broadcasting sports before 7pm initially (to protect newspaper sales!)
  - Sports broadcasting drove early adoption of radio, TV, color TV, streaming
- **For our essay:** Sports didn't just benefit from broadcasting — they drove its adoption. Sports are the original "killer app" for every new media platform.

### 5. Wikipedia: "List of most-watched television broadcasts"
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_most-watched_television_broadcasts
- **Key data:**
  - Most-watched events globally: FIFA World Cup, Summer Olympics, Cricket World Cup
  - Most-watched annual events: CMG New Year's Gala (China), Super Bowl (US), IPL (India), UEFA Champions League (Europe)
  - FIFA World Cup 2022 final: estimated 1.5B viewers globally (unverified)
  - 2010 Winter Olympics hockey final: 26.5M Canadians watched part of it (80% of population!)
  - Independent analysis suggests few broadcasts actually reach 1B viewers despite claims
  - Note: "Figures published by organisers and journalists are routinely exaggerated"
- **For our essay:** Even with caveats, the numbers dwarf anything Rome could achieve. Circus Maximus held 150K physically; a single World Cup final reaches hundreds of millions simultaneously.

## Esports (The Newest Branch)

### 7. Wikipedia: "Esports"
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Esports
- **Key data:**
  - First competition: 1972 at Stanford University (Spacewar!)
  - First large-scale: 1980 Space Invaders Championship (10,000+ participants)
  - By 2020: estimated 454 million viewers, revenue >$1 billion
  - China: 35% of global esports revenue
  - **Olympic Esports Games set for 2027** — formal recognition by IOC
  - Genres: MOBA, FPS, fighting, battle royale, RTS, card games
  - Key tournaments: League of Legends World Championship, Dota 2 International (prize pool peaked >$40M)
  - Early 2010s audience: 85% male, ages 18-34
- **For our essay:** Esports is the most dramatic example of "branching" — an entirely new category of sport that didn't exist 50 years ago. Rome couldn't conceive of non-physical competition as spectacle. The fact that IOC is considering Olympic inclusion shows sport's definition itself is evolving.

## Sports Betting (Modern-Only Metric)

### 8. Wikipedia: "Sports betting"
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sports_betting
- **Key data:**
  - US sports betting legalized in many states after PASPA overturned in 2018
  - Global sports betting market estimated at $200-300B+ annually
  - Fantasy sports: considered "skill games," 32+ US states have allowed/regulated
  - Bet types: moneyline, spread, over/under, props, futures, parlays
  - Romans also bet on gladiator outcomes — but informally, not a commercialized industry
- **For our essay:** Betting existed in Rome (informal wagers on gladiators) but never became a structured industry. Modern sports betting is now a multi-hundred-billion dollar industry that adds an entire economic layer the Romans never had.

## Olympic Growth (Structured Data)

### 9. Wikipedia: "Summer Olympic Games"
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer_Olympic_Games
- **Key data:**
  - 1896 Athens: 42 events, 245 competitors, 14 nations, men only
  - 1900 Paris: first women competitors (22 women, 5 sports)
  - 2020 Tokyo: 339 events, 11,319 competitors, 206 nations, ~48% women
  - 2024 Paris: 329 events, ~10,500 athletes, 206 nations, 32 sports
  - Growth: 42 → 339 events (8x), 245 → 11,319 athletes (46x), 14 → 206 nations (15x)
  - 19 different host countries across 5 continents
  - Only comparison: Ancient Olympics ran 776 BC — 393 AD (~1,169 years) in ONE location (Olympia), men only, Greek-speaking world only
- **For our essay:** The Olympic data is the clearest "branching" metric over time. A single chart showing events/athletes/nations from 1896-2024 tells the whole story.

## Metrics That Didn't Exist in Roman Era

These are modern-only dimensions worth mentioning in the essay:

| Modern Metric | Why It's New |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Broadcasting** | No electronic media; spectacle was purely physical attendance |
| **Athlete agency** | Gladiators were mostly slaves/prisoners; modern athletes are free agents |
| **Women's participation** | Roman spectacle was male-dominated; modern Olympics are ~50/50 |
| **Commercial funding** | Roman games were state/patron funded; modern sports are commercially driven |
| **Sports betting industry** | Roman wagering was informal; modern is a $200B+ structured industry |
| **Esports** | Non-physical competitive spectacle was inconceivable |
| **Fantasy sports** | Participatory spectatorship — fans become quasi-managers |
| **Athlete compensation transparency** | Roman gladiator pay was ad hoc; modern contracts are public record |
| **Player unions/labor rights** | Gladiators had no collective bargaining |
| **Global participation** | Rome was 1 empire; modern sports span 200+ nations |
